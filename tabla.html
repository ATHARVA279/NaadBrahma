TABLA

Origin

  • The tabla is a traditional percussion instrument that has its origins in India.
  • It is an integral part of Indian classical music, and its history can be traced back over several centuries.
  • The exact origin of the tabla is somewhat debated, but it is generally believed to have evolved from a variety of hand-drums and percussion instruments that were used in different regions of India.
  • The tabla as we know it today is thought to have emerged during the medieval period.

Tabla Gharanas and Styles

  • The tabla is deeply rooted in Indian classical music, with different regions giving rise to distinctive playing styles or gharanas.
  • Prominent gharanas include the Delhi, Ajrara, Farukhabad, Benaras, and Punjab styles, each with its unique characteristics.
  • These gharanas are a testament to the diversity and richness of tabla playing techniques and traditions.
  • Tabla players often identify with a specific gharana and receive training in its unique approach.
